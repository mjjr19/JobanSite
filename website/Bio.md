Sheikh Mohamad Joban is a respected Muslim scholar, educator, and community leader who has dedicated over 30 years to serving the Muslim community in Washington State. Known for his warmth, humor, and practical approach to teaching Islam, he has become one of the most influential Islamic figures in the Pacific Northwest.

Education & Islamic Training

Sheikh Joban began his formal Islamic studies in Indonesia, earning a B.A. in Arabic from the Islamic University in Jakarta. He then continued his education at Al-Azhar University in Cairo, where he completed:

B.A. in Theology, Islamic Call, and Culture

M.A. in Islamic Studies

His training at one of the world’s leading centers of Islamic scholarship shaped the foundation of his lifelong work in da‘wah.

Service in the United States

Since arriving in America in the early 1990s, Sheikh Joban has been deeply involved in teaching, counseling, and community leadership. He served over 20 years as the full-time Imam of the Islamic Center of Olympia, where his khutbahs, classes, and mentorship inspired thousands.

Today, he continues to serve the community in several key roles:

President of the Imam Fatwa Committee of Washington State

Muslim Chaplain for the Washington State Department of Corrections

Instructor at the Internet Islamic University

In 2003, Sheikh Joban was honored with leading a prayer at the Washington State House of Representatives, marking a historic moment for the Muslim community.

Dawah, Teaching & Community Impact

Sheikh Joban’s lectures cover Qur’anic tafsir, seerah, fiqh, character development, family guidance, and youth education. He is frequently invited to speak at mosques, conferences, universities, and interfaith events across the region.

Through his YouTube lectures and online teaching, he continues to reach a global audience with clear, accessible Islamic knowledge.

Author of Kiprah Dakwah (Now in English)

Sheikh Joban is the author of Kiprah Dakwah, a memoir reflecting on 30 years of da‘wah in the United States. The newly released English edition shares stories of those who embraced Islam, lessons from community service, and insights into the challenges and blessings of spreading Islam in America.

Personal Life

Sheikh Joban lives in Redmond, Washington, with his wife and twin children. His approachable character, dedication to serving others, and passion for teaching continue to inspire people across generations.
